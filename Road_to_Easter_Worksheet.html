📖 Let's Read the Story First!

Day 1 — Palm Sunday
🤴 The King is Here!
Jesus rode into the city on a little donkey. Everyone was SO happy! They waved green palm branches and shouted "Hosanna!" — which means "Save us!"
📖 "Hosanna! Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord!" — John 12:13
🍞
Day 2 — Maundy Thursday
🍽️ The Special Supper
Jesus had a last dinner with His friends. He washed their feet to show how to be kind and humble. Then they shared bread and wine together.
📖 "Love one another. As I have loved you..." — John 13:34
✝️
Day 3 — Good Friday
💜 Jesus Shows His Great Love
This is a quiet, sad day. Jesus died on a cross. We call it "Good Friday" because Jesus did it to show the biggest love in the whole world for us!
📖 "God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son." — John 3:16
🪨
Day 4 — Holy Saturday
🌙 The Quiet Waiting
Jesus' body was placed in a tomb. A big, heavy rock was put in front of it. Everything was very still. Everyone was waiting and wondering...
📖 "Wait for the Lord; be strong and take heart." — Psalm 27:14
🌟
Day 5 — Easter Sunday 🎉
🎊 The BEST Surprise! Jesus is RISEN!
On Sunday morning, the stone was rolled away! An angel appeared and said: "Don't be afraid! He is not here — He has RISEN!" Jesus is alive and lives in our hearts today. HAPPY EASTER! 🐣🌸
📖 "He is not here; he has risen, just as he said!" — Matthew 28:6
"He is not here; He has risen, just as He said!" 🌟 — Matthew 28:6 (The Best News Ever!)

1 🐴 On Palm Sunday, what animal did Jesus ride into the city?
A
🐎 A big, fast horse
B
🐴 A little donkey
C
🐘 A huge elephant
D
🦁 A lion
✅ Answer: B — A little donkey!
Jesus chose to ride a small, gentle donkey — not a big war horse. This showed He was a humble King of Peace, not a warrior king. It was a very special moment! The people were so happy they waved palm branches and shouted "Hosanna!" (which means "Save us!"). This is why we celebrate Palm Sunday! 🌿
2 📣 What did the people shout when Jesus came into the city?
A
🙅 "Go away!"
B
🤫 "Be quiet, Jesus!"
C
🙌 "Hosanna! Blessed is He!"
D
😴 "We are sleeping!"
✅ Answer: C — "Hosanna! Blessed is He who comes in the name of the Lord!"
"Hosanna" is a special Hebrew word that means "Save us!" or "Please help us!" The people were celebrating and praising Jesus because they believed He was the King sent by God. They were SO happy and excited! 🎉
📖 John 12:13
3 🍽️ On Maundy Thursday, what did Jesus do for His friends at dinner?
A
🎂 He baked them a birthday cake
B
🧹 He cleaned the whole house
C
🦶 He washed their feet
D
🎁 He gave them presents
✅ Answer: C — He washed their feet!
In Jesus' time, washing someone's feet was a job for servants. But Jesus — the King! — got down and washed His friends' feet. He was teaching them (and us!) an important lesson: true love means serving others, even if it is a humble job. That's what "Maundy" means — it comes from the word "mandate" (command): Love one another! 💛
📖 John 13:34
4 ✝️ Why do we call it "Good" Friday even though it is a sad day?
A
😊 Because everyone was happy that day
B
💖 Because Jesus showed the biggest love by dying for us
C
🌞 Because the weather was very good
D
🎈 Because people had a big party
✅ Answer: B — Because Jesus showed the biggest love!
Even though Jesus dying on the cross is very sad, Christians call it "Good Friday" because of what it means — Jesus gave His life to show how much God loves every single person in the world. It is the greatest act of love ever! That is why it is called "GOOD." ❤️
📖 John 3:16 — "God so loved the world that He gave His one and only Son."
5 🪨 On Holy Saturday, where was Jesus' body placed?
A
🏠 In a house
B
🌊 In the river
C
⛰️ In a tomb (a cave), with a big rock in front
D
🌳 Under a big tree
✅ Answer: C — In a tomb (a cave), with a big rock in front!
A tomb is like a cave carved into rock, used as a resting place. After Jesus died, His body was placed inside a tomb, and a very big, heavy rock was rolled in front of the door. Everyone was waiting and wondering what would happen next... It was a very quiet, still day. 🌙
📖 Psalm 27:14 — "Wait for the Lord; be strong and take heart."
6 👼 On Easter Sunday morning, what did the angel say?
A
😴 "Jesus is still sleeping!"
B
🚶 "Jesus went for a walk!"
C
✨ "Don't be afraid! He is not here — He has RISEN!"
D
🤔 "We don't know where Jesus is."
✅ Answer: C — "Don't be afraid! He is not here — He has RISEN!"
On Easter Sunday morning, the big stone was rolled away! When Jesus' friends came to the tomb, they found it EMPTY. An angel (a messenger from God) appeared and said those wonderful words. "Risen" means Jesus came back to life! This is the GREATEST news in the whole Bible! That's why Easter is the most important celebration for Christians! 🎉🌸
📖 Matthew 28:6 — "He is not here; he has risen, just as he said!"

Part A — Multiple Choice (6 points)
Q1. On Palm Sunday, what animal did Jesus ride?
✅ B — A little donkey 🐴
The Bible (John 12:15) says Jesus rode a young donkey. This fulfilled an old prophecy that the King would come "gentle and riding on a donkey." Choosing a small, humble animal — not a war horse — showed Jesus was the Prince of Peace. This is a key concept for Grade 1: Jesus was a different kind of king — a loving, humble king.
📖 John 12:13-15
Q2. What did the people shout when Jesus came?
✅ C — "Hosanna! Blessed is He!" 🙌
"Hosanna" (הוֹשַׁנָּא) is from Hebrew, meaning "Please save!" or "Save now!" The crowd was praising Jesus as a King and Savior. Help the student practise saying "Hosanna" out loud — it's a fun word to say! You can even act it out: have the child wave their arms like they're holding palm branches.
📖 John 12:13
Q3. What did Jesus do for His friends on Maundy Thursday?
✅ C — He washed their feet 🦶
This is the most counter-cultural action in the story. In ancient times, washing feet was a servant's job. Jesus — the Lord and Teacher — did this humble act to demonstrate servanthood and love. The word "Maundy" comes from Latin "mandatum" meaning "commandment" — Jesus commanded: "Love one another." Key takeaway for kids: being kind, even in small ways, is being like Jesus.
📖 John 13:34
Q4. Why is it called "Good Friday" even though it's sad?
✅ B — Because Jesus showed the biggest love 💖
"Good" in Old English also meant "holy" or "sacred." Good Friday is considered "good" because of the profound gift of love — Jesus sacrificing himself so that people could be forgiven and connected to God. For Grade 1, keep it simple: "Jesus loved us SO much, He did the hardest thing ever." Emphasize love, not the violence.
📖 John 3:16
Q5. Where was Jesus' body placed on Holy Saturday?
✅ C — In a tomb (cave) with a big rock in front 🪨
A tomb in Jesus' time was a cave-like space cut into rock, sealed with a large circular stone. Roman soldiers even guarded it. Holy Saturday is a day of waiting and stillness — a good lesson about patience. Compare it to waiting for a surprise — the disciples didn't know what would happen next!
📖 Psalm 27:14
Q6. What did the angel say on Easter Sunday morning?
✅ C — "Don't be afraid! He is not here — He has RISEN!" 👼
This is the central message of Easter — the Resurrection. "Risen" means Jesus came back to life. When Jesus' followers found the empty tomb, the angel reassured them with this joyful announcement. This is the core of Christian faith: Jesus is ALIVE. Encourage the child to memorise Matthew 28:6 — "He has risen, just as He said!" It's short and powerful!
📖 Matthew 28:6
Part B — True or False (4 points)
Q7. People waved palm branches when Jesus came. TRUE or FALSE?
✅ TRUE 🌿
TRUE. The crowd waved and laid down palm branches — a sign of honour and celebration, like a royal welcome. That's why this day is called "Palm Sunday" and churches often hand out palm crosses on this day.
Q8. At the special supper, they shared bread and juice. TRUE or FALSE?
❌ FALSE 🍷
FALSE. They shared bread and wine, not juice. This "Last Supper" is remembered in churches through Holy Communion (also called Eucharist or Lord's Supper). In some churches children drink grape juice instead, so this distinction is worth discussing gently at the right age.
Q9. "Good Friday" was a fun party day. TRUE or FALSE?
❌ FALSE 💜
FALSE. Good Friday is a solemn, quiet day of reflection. It is the day Jesus was crucified. It is called "GOOD" because of the salvific meaning behind Jesus' sacrifice — not because it was a joyful occasion. Churches often hold quiet services on this day.
Q10. On Easter Sunday, the tomb was empty. TRUE or FALSE?
✅ TRUE 🌟
TRUE! The empty tomb is the most important symbol of Easter. Jesus rose from the dead — this event is called the Resurrection and it is the foundation of Christian faith. Celebrate this one enthusiastically with the student — it's THE GOOD NEWS! 🎉
Part C — Fill in the Blank (4 points)
Q11–14. Fill in the blanks.
11: donkey 🐴  |  12: Hosanna 📣  |  13: feet 🦶  |  14: angel 👼
• Donkey (Q11) — The key word from Palm Sunday.
• Hosanna (Q12) — The special word of praise; good vocabulary for Grade 1.
• Feet (Q13) — The servant act of Maundy Thursday.
• Angel (Q14) — The messenger of the Resurrection on Easter morning.

The decoy words (cross, tomb) are also important story vocabulary — use them to extend the discussion! "Where did you see the word 'cross' in the story?" etc.
Part D — Matching (5 points)
Q15–19. Match each Holy Week day to its event.
🌿 Palm Sunday → Jesus rode a donkey 🐴
🍽️ Maundy Thursday → Jesus washed feet & had supper 🦶🍞
✝️ Good Friday → Jesus died on the cross 💜
🪨 Holy Saturday → Jesus' body was in the tomb 🌙
🌟 Easter Sunday → Jesus rose from the dead! 🐣
For Grade 1, the sequence/order of these events is crucial. Tip: Use your fingers and count them out: "Palm Sunday (1 finger), Maundy Thursday (2 fingers)..." — this helps the child remember the chronological flow of Holy Week. You can also draw a simple "road" timeline on paper and place each event in order!
Part E — Drawing Activity (1 point)
Q20. Draw your favourite part of the Easter story.
✅ 1 point — Any honest attempt earns full marks! 🎨
This is an open-ended creative activity. Any drawing that relates to the Easter story earns the point. The goal is to see which part of the story resonated most with the child, and to discuss it together. Common answers might include: the donkey, the palm branches, the empty tomb, or the angel. Use the child's drawing as a springboard for conversation: "Why did you draw this? What happened in that part?"
